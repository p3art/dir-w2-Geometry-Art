大馬士革謝赫·穆希丁·伊本·阿拉比陵墓及聖陵的一塊牆板上發現了六邊形圖案。該陵墓由蘇丹塞利姆一世（公元 1470-1520 年）建造，他是奧斯曼帝國的締造者和統治者（1512 年至 1520 年），並於 1516-17 年征服了大馬士革。

圖案分析來源：[Aslam Qureshi]
照片圖片來源：SYR_0222（David Wade 檔案）

一段歷史敘述講述了蘇菲派神秘主義者、學者兼哲學家伊本·阿拉比（公元 1165-1240 年）的故事。伊本·阿拉比以其關於宇宙學、形而上學和「存在統一性」（Wahdat al-Wujud）概念的著作而聞名。他曾預言，一個名叫塞利姆的人終有一天會征服大馬士革，並找到他隱藏的陵墓。正如預言所言，這座陵墓在薩利希耶區被發現，蘇丹塞利姆隨即著手修建一座由塔基亞（takiyya）、陵墓和神殿組成的清真寺建築群，並將其獻給伊本·阿拉比（Ibn al-Arabi）。伊本·阿拉比後來被稱為謝赫·阿克巴爾（al-Shaykh al-Akbar，最偉大的謝赫）和穆赫伊丁（Muḥyiddīn，阿拉伯語：محيي الدين，宗教復興者），並被尊崇為聖人。

在安納托利亞科尼亞的錫爾恰利清真寺（Sirçali Cami）、保加爾清真寺（Bulgar Tekke Cami）和薩希普·阿塔清真寺（Sahip Ata Cami）中也發現了相當相似的13世紀六重圖案。西元1210年，伊本·阿拉比曾在此地度過了一年，從事教學和撰寫註釋。

A hexagonal pattern found on a wall panel in the Sheikh Muhyiddin Ibn Arabi mausoleum and shrine in Damascus built by Sultan Selim I (CE 1470-1520), founder and ruler of the Ottoman Empire from 1512 to 1520, who conquered Damascus in 1516-17.

A historical narrative tells that the Sufi mystic, scholar and philosopher Ibn al-Arabi (CE 1165-1240), known for his writings on cosmology, metaphysics, and the concept of "Wahdat al-Wujud" (Unity of Being), had prophesied that a man named Selim would one day conquer Damascus and find his hidden tomb. True to the prophecy, the tomb was found in the Salihiyah district and Sultan Selim began at once to construct a mosque complex comprised of a takiyya, mausoleum and shrine, which he dedicated to Ibn al-Arabi, who later became known and revered as al-Shaykh al-Akbar (The Greatest Sheikh) and Muḥyiddīn (Arabic: محيي الدين; The Reviver of Religion).

Rather similar 13th century sixfold patterns are found in the Sirçali Cami, Bulgar Tekke Cami and Sahip Ata Cami in Konya, Anatolia, where in CE 1210 Ibn ‘Arabi spent a year teaching and writing commentaries.
